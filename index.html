About Us

Historically, every village in the country had a panchayat which was responsible for finding the solutions to the local problems within the village itself. Panchayats or the village councils are as old as India's history and have been a part of her tradition. In Discovery of India, Pandit Nehru has provided a fairly exhaustive idea about the working of the Village Panchayats in ancient India. Panchayats had vast powers, both executive and judicial. Its members were treated with great respect by the king's courtiers and the public. Land was allotted by the panchayats. They also collected taxes out of agricultural produce and paid the share of the village to the kings. Over and above the Village Panchayats/Village Councils, there was a big panchayat to supervise and intervene, if necessary.
